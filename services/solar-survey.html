Solar Survey and Installation

Solar survey or site survey for solar installation refers to analyzing the site before installing a solar panel. A site survey is required in order to build a proper and lucrative solar energy producing system for the system owner. The position of solar panels, their orientation, and the quantity of sunlight available all have a part in deciding how much energy you can create from the sun. Major parameters for Solar Installation:

Major parameters for Solar Installation:

Climatic Condition of the site

The degree of solar irradiation, temperature, and wind speed changes at the location offer an assessment of the potential for the solar PV project and the individual components required. For example, low sun irradiation areas necessitate a more efficient solar panel than high solar irradiation sites. Similarly, solar panels perform better in cooler climates than in warm climates. In addition, the installation design of a solar system should take into account the worst wind load on the panels and the structure they are installed on.

Property type/ roof

The structure of the property and the kind of roof dictate the design of the solar system, therefore defining the type of property as residential, commercial, industrial, or institutional is critical. The type of roof is also significant, since there are several options, including RCC, metal sheet, aluminum sheet, and asbestos. A roof can be flat or sloping, with a specified capacity to support the weight of panels, which helps to define many other elements that are reliant on this knowledge.

Location of solar PV array and Shade Analysis

During the site survey, it is critical to select the optimal solar PV array. Solar PV arrays can be built in three orientations on the property: south, south-east, and south-west. The solar PV array should be built in a position that is free of shadows. Obstructions such as nearby buildings, trees, water tanks, dish antennas, parapet walls, and other structures should be identified during the site inspection since they might generate shadow, which can reduce energy output. Throughout the year, shadow analysis is performed to guarantee that the maximum amount of sunshine is caught between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 3:00 p.m.

Space availability

A 1kW solar system requires 80 square feet of area. So, for a 10kW system, you'll need 800 square feet. During a site survey, the potential area on the roof or on the ground is measured, and the solar PV system is developed accordingly. The structure and kind of roof (flat or slope), as well as its orientation, adjacent obstacles, and accessibility, all influence the position of a solar PV array.

Location for mounting solar system components

The position and layout of mounting additional components must be indicated in the site survey once the best location for the PV installation has been determined. Factors like the distribution box, the inverter, and the whole system's wiring routing should all be determined. If the installation is off-grid, the battery must be installed as well.
